# Reviewer Diagnostics

This note documents the additional diagnostics added after the final reviewer-oriented statistical audit. These outputs do not change the primary estimand or the main causal analysis; they make the temporal structure, matched population, non-matched population, and missing-data support transparent.

## Comment 2: Operator and Calendar-Time Structure

The repository now exports the distribution of ATRACT and conventional traction procedures by operator stratum and study year, together with crude dissection-speed summaries.

Primary outputs:

- `results/tables/table_s10_operator_year_distribution.csv`: procedure counts, ATRACT proportion, and crude speed summaries by operator stratum and study year.
- `results/tables/table_s11_operator_adoption.csv`: first ATRACT study year per operator stratum and counts before/from adoption.
- `results/figures/figure_s6_operator_year_treatment.{png,pdf,svg}`: operator-year ATRACT use.
- `results/figures/figure_s7_operator_year_speed.{png,pdf,svg}`: crude median speed by operator-year cell.

Temporal sensitivity outputs:

- `results/tables/table_s6_speed_temporal_sensitivity.csv`: speed effects under calendar-proximity and post-adoption diagnostics.
- `results/model_summaries/speed_temporal_sensitivity.csv`: machine-readable version of the same estimates.
- `results/model_summaries/speed_temporal_rematching_grid.csv`: rematching grid with year-gap restriction.
- `results/model_summaries/speed_contemporary_rematching_grid.csv`: rematching grid restricted to the post-adoption period.
- `results/model_summaries/speed_temporal_relaxation_grid.csv`: speed estimates and large-lesion retention under progressively relaxed calendar-gap constraints.
- `results/tables/table_s15_speed_temporal_relaxation_grid.csv`: manuscript-facing version of the temporal-relaxation grid.

Interpretation for the manuscript:

- Calendar time and operator are not ignored; they are handled in the propensity score and examined in supplementary diagnostics.
- Relaxing the calendar-gap constraint can be reported as a sensitivity analysis; it should not be tuned on p-values.
- The post-adoption diagnostic is descriptive/sensitivity evidence, not a replacement for the primary matched analysis.
- `operator_other` is explicitly identified as a grouped heterogeneous stratum and should be discussed as a limitation.

## Comment 7: Matched and Non-Matched Populations

The repository now exports treatment-group characteristics for the primary matched speed cohort and a profile of speed-support procedures not retained after matching.

Primary outputs:

- `results/tables/table_s12_matched_characteristics.csv`: matched-cohort characteristics by treatment group.
- `results/tables/table_s13_unmatched_profile.csv`: characteristics of the speed-support procedures not retained in the primary 1:1 match.
- `results/tables/table_s1_balance.csv`: SMD-based balance diagnostics before and after matching.

Current counts:

- Primary matched speed cohort: 782 procedures, 391 conventional traction and 391 ATRACT.
- Not retained from the speed-support cohort after matching: 1134 procedures, 1043 conventional traction and 91 ATRACT.

Interpretation for the manuscript:

- The SMD table documents balance; the matched-characteristics table documents the actual values by group.
- The unmatched-profile table should be used to describe what the matched estimand does not cover.
- The main claim should remain restricted to comparable matched cases, not the entire raw cohort.

## Comment 8: Missingness and Patient-Level Clustering

The repository now exports missingness by variable and by treatment group. Patient-level clustering cannot be evaluated from the public analytic CSV because patient identifiers are deliberately excluded.

Public outputs:

- `results/tables/table_s4_missingness.csv`: missingness by variable.
- `results/tables/table_s14_missingness_by_group.csv`: missingness by variable and treatment group.
- `results/tables/table_s5_population_accounting.csv`: complete-case and support-restriction counts by endpoint.

Private aggregate output:

- `results/model_summaries/patient_multiplicity_private_summary.csv`: non-identifying patient-multiplicity summary generated from the private source workbook using `make private-diagnostics`.

Current private aggregate finding:

- Cleaned cohort: 2003 procedures and 2002 unique patient identifiers.
- One patient contributed two procedures.
- Primary matched speed cohort: no repeated patient identifier.

Interpretation for the manuscript:

- Patient-level clustering is empirically negligible in the current cleaned cohort.
- Because the primary matched speed cohort has no repeated patient identifier, matched-set clustered inference remains appropriate for the primary analysis.
- Patient identifiers must not be added to the public dataset.

## Reproducibility

The added outputs are generated by:

```bash
make analysis
ATRACT_RAW_XLSX=/absolute/path/to/base24.xlsx make private-diagnostics
```

The private diagnostic target writes only aggregate counts and does not export patient identifiers.
